How To Pick The Right Color Furniture

CONSIDER THE EXISTING COLOUR SCHEMA

Wood has beautiful grain patterns and textures which make it stand out, compared to other furniture materials. That’s why it never goes out of style. Applying the ideal wood paint can highlight its patterns and make the wooden furniture look even more exquisite. Some of them provide excellent grain clarity and retain the gloss level or sheen of the furniture for years.

CONSIDER THE SIZE OF THE ROOM

The size of your room is one of the most important factors to consider when selecting furniture colours. The right colour can make a small room appear larger. The wrong colour can make a large room feel empty and uninviting. Using colour psychology, certain hues can evoke emotions and create a sense of space. Lighter shades like white, beige, and pastels can make a room feel open and airy. Darker colours like navy blue, charcoal, and black can make a room feel smaller and cosy.

THINK ABOUT THE MOOD YOU WANT TO CREATE

The colours you choose for your piece of furniture can have a huge impact on the overall vibe of the room. Whether you’re looking for a calm and peaceful space or a lively and energetic atmosphere, the right colours can make all the difference. Start by identifying the purpose of the room. Is it a bedroom where you want to feel relaxed and calm or a living room where you want to be energized and social? When you have a picture of the mood of the room, align it with colour psychology. Different colours carry varying visual weights and can elicit different emotions and moods.

THINK ABOUT NATURAL LIGHT

Natural light can have a significant impact on the way colours appear in a room. The amount and direction of light can make colours look warmer or cooler, brighter or darker. Wondering how to choose furniture colours that look best in your room’s natural light? Start by taking note of the lighting intensity. Suppose your room has a lot of natural light. Consider cooler furniture tones like greens. These colours will make it appear brighter and fresher. However, if your room lacks natural light, you may want to opt for warm colours, like reds and yellows. These colours will add warmth and depth to the space.
